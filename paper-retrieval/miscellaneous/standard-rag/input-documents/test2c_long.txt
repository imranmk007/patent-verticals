The patch integrates a multi-wavelength near-infrared LED array (1100–1650 nm) with a matched InGaAs photodetector in a diffuse reflectance geometry, positioned against the inner forearm for consistent optical coupling. Tissue scattering is decoupled from absorption through spatially offset source-detector separations (1–8 mm), isolating dermal and subcutaneous sampling volumes where interstitial glucose correlates reliably with blood glucose. Raw spectral data are processed onboard by a low-power Cortex-M4 DSP running a partial least squares regression model, with a modified Beer-Lambert correction accounting for tissue inhomogeneity, temperature drift, and motion artifact via a triaxial accelerometer feed. Calibration is factory-seeded using population-derived spectral priors and personalized through a single fingerstick at device initialization. Clinical advantages include elimination of transcutaneous needle insertion, reduced infection risk, anticoagulant therapy compatibility, and sustained wear beyond 14 days without sensor degradation or adhesive-related skin compromise.
