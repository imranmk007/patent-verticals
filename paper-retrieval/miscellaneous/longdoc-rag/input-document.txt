TITLE: AUTOMATED RECIRCULATING HYDROPONIC CULTIVATION APPARATUS WITH ENHANCED NUTRIENT-SENSING LOGIC AND MODULAR LED SPECTRUM ARRAYS
FIELD OF THE INVENTION
The present invention relates generally to precision agricultural systems and automated indoor cultivation infrastructure. More particularly, the disclosure relates to an industrial-grade, closed-loop recirculating hydroponic cultivation apparatus utilizing advanced nutrient-sensing logic, non-invasive electrochemical sensor arrays, and dynamically programmable modular light-emitting diode (LED) spectrum configurations to optimize vegetative and generative plant growth cycles while minimizing water and nutrient expenditure.
BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION
Traditional agricultural practices are heavily dependent on unpredictable environmental variables such as seasonal sunlight, ambient temperature fluctuations, and variable soil composition. In response to these vulnerabilities, controlled environment agriculture (CEA) and vertical farming frameworks have emerged. Hydroponic cultivation represents a key subset of CEA, wherein plants are grown without soil substrate, instead relying on aqueous solutions containing dissolved essential minerals and micronutrients.
Despite the theoretical efficiencies of hydroponic farming, current commercial systems exhibit significant operational limitations. Conventional automated setups generally rely on static, time-scheduled irrigation intervals and rudimentary chemical dosing pumps. These pumps typically inject pre-mixed chemical concentrates based on coarse measurements of Electrical Conductivity (EC) and Hydrogen Ion Concentration (pH). However, standard EC tracking represents a cumulative metric of total dissolved solids; it completely fails to differentiate between individual ionic concentrations, such as nitrates (NO₃⁻), phosphates (H₂PO₄⁻), and potassium ions (K⁺). Consequently, certain macronutrients become critically depleted while others accumulate to phytotoxic thresholds, forcing operators to execute frequent, resource-intensive reservoir flushes.
Furthermore, conventional indoor lighting architectures rely on static spectrum outputs. While specific spectral wavelengths (such as deep red at 660 nm for chlorophyll A activation and deep blue at 450 nm for chlorophyll B activation) are critical for photosynthetic efficiency, plants require dynamically shifting spectral distributions at different phenological stages (e.g., vegetative propagation versus floral induction). Fixed lighting rigs fail to adapt to these shifting biological demands, yielding suboptimal photon conversion efficiencies and excessive thermal generation.
Accordingly, there exists an immediate and critical need in the art of automated agriculture for an integrated, closed-loop hydroponic apparatus capable of real-time individual ion tracking, autonomous algorithmic nutrient balancing, and targeted spectral delivery to maximize crop yields while preserving system resources.
DETAILED DESCRIPTION OF THE PREFERRED EMBODIMENTS
The following detailed description references an exemplary embodiment of the automated recirculating hydroponic cultivation apparatus. It is to be understood that alternative structural embodiments and programmatic variations may be deployed without departing from the scope of the present invention.
Structural Framework and Aqueous Architecture
The apparatus comprises a primary multi-tier vertical cultivation rack constructed from powder-coated, food-grade structural aluminum extrusions. Each cultivation tier supports a specialized grow channel or basin designed to manage flow characteristics. The basins are sloped at a precise gradient of 1:30 to 1:40 relative to the horizontal plane to ensure uniform gravitational fluid return without stagnation points. Plants are secured within individual high-density polyethylene (HDPE) net cups containing an inert, highly porous root-zone substrate such as expanded clay pebbles or horticultural rockwool.
Positioned beneath the vertical cultivation matrix is a primary fluid reservoir with an operational capacity scaling from 200 to 1000 liters depending on the system configuration. This reservoir is constructed from opaque, ultra-violet stabilized polypropylene to eliminate light penetration and entirely prevent opportunistic algal proliferation. The fluid architecture operates as a strictly closed-loop recirculating system. Aqueous nutrient delivery is driven by a variable-speed, brushless direct-current (BLDC) submersible pump rated for continuous duty cycles. This pump forces the nutrient solution through an inline ultra-filtration assembly consisting of a 50-micron mechanical particulate screen followed by a low-pressure ultraviolet (UV-C) sterilization chamber operating at a wavelength of 254 nanometers. This sterilization loop actively neutralizes waterborne bacterial pathogens, fungal spores, and pythium oospores before the fluid interfaces with the crop root zones.
Advanced Sensor Array and Dosing Matrix
The primary structural and fluidic innovation of the present invention resides within the inline sensor manifold located downstream from the UV sterilization loop. Unlike baseline systems that utilize simple bulk EC probes, the sensor manifold of this apparatus embeds a microfluidic ion-selective electrode (ISE) array. This array is equipped with solid-state polymer membranes calibrated to isolate and quantify the specific electromotive force generated by distinct ionic species: namely, Nitrate (NO₃⁻), Ammonium (NH₄⁺), Orthophosphate (H₂PO₄⁻), Potassium (K⁺), Calcium (Ca²⁺), and Magnesium (Mg²⁺).
The analog voltage outputs from the ISE array are continuously fed into a high-resolution 16-bit analog-to-digital converter (ADC) integrated into the system's central microcontroller unit (MCU). The MCU runs a local deterministic control algorithm that compares real-time ionic ratios against a multi-dimensional crop-specific target matrix stored in non-volatile flash memory.
When the real-time concentration of a specific ion deviates by more than a pre-programmed threshold (typically +/- 5% from target PPM values), the MCU sends pulse-width modulation (PWM) signals to an array of high-precision, medical-grade peristaltic dosing pumps. These dosing pumps are directly connected to isolated concentrate storage vessels containing highly concentrated, single-element liquid nutrients. For example, if the ISE array detects an accelerated uptake of nitrates during a rapid vegetative growth burst, the system isolates this specific deficit and activates only the nitrate concentrate pump, rather than injecting a bulk N-P-K mixture that would inadvertently spike phosphorus and potassium to toxic levels.
Simultaneously, a dual-junction glass electrode pH sensor continuously monitors the log-inverse hydrogen ion activity of the solution. If the pH shifts outside the optimal physiological envelope of 5.8 to 6.2, the system engages dedicated acid (0.1M Phosphoric Acid) or base (0.1M Potassium Hydroxide) peristaltic pumps to restore equilibrium via a proportional-integral-derivative (PID) feedback loop.
Dynamic Modular LED Spectrum Arrays
Suspended directly above each cultivation channel is a modular, high-efficiency solid-state lighting canopy. Each canopy features an engineered matrix of surface-mount device (SMD) LEDs capable of emitting discrete, narrow-band spectral peaks. The integrated LED channels include:
Deep Blue Channel: Peak wavelength emissions strictly targeted at 450 nanometers.
Hyper Red Channel: Peak wavelength emissions strictly targeted at 660 nanometers.
Far Red Channel: Peak wavelength emissions strictly targeted at 730 nanometers.
Broad-Spectrum White Channel: High Color Rendering Index (CRI > 90) baseline operating at a correlated color temperature of 3000 Kelvin.
The illumination matrix is controlled via a multi-channel digital dimming driver communicating via the I²C protocol with the central MCU. The apparatus implements an automated photoperiod and spectral tuning schedule tailored to the specific phenological phase of the target crop.
During the initial vegetative propagation phase, the MCU drives the 450 nm blue channel at peak current density while attenuating the 660 nm red channel. This spectral profile mimics early spring sunlight, promoting thick stem development, tight internodal spacing, and expansive leaf surface area while suppressing premature bolting. Upon transition to the generative or flowering phase, the MCU automatically executes a pre-programmed script that flips the spectral balance: the 450 nm blue channel is throttled down to a maintenance level, while the 660 nm Hyper Red and 730 nm Far Red channels are driven to maximum intensity. This specific combination triggers the phytochrome photochemistry required to maximize floral site differentiation, accelerate biomass accumulation, and stimulate the synthesis of target secondary metabolites.
To prevent thermal stress on the plant canopy and extend the operational lifespan of the solid-state emitters, each lighting module is thermally bonded to an advanced anodized aluminum passive heat sink utilizing high-conductivity phase-change thermal interface materials. This eliminates the need for mechanical cooling fans, removing a common point of mechanical failure and drastically lowering the acoustic profile of the cultivation facility.
Algorithmic Logic and Data Orchestration
The operational backbone of the apparatus is a 32-bit ARM Cortex-M4 microcontroller running a real-time operating system (RTOS). The firmware executes three parallel, isolated software tasks:
The Sensor Polling Task: Samples the ISE array, pH probe, dissolved oxygen (DO) meter, and fluid temperature sensors at a deterministic frequency of 0.1 Hz (once every 10 seconds). Digital filtering algorithms, including moving-average and Kalman filters, are applied to the raw data stream to eliminate high-frequency signal noise generated by the nearby BLDC pump motors.
The Actuator Control Task: Evaluates the filtered sensor outputs against the target crop profile. This task utilizes predictive feed-forward modeling to compute the exact volumetric injection requirements for the peristaltic dosing pumps, adjusting for fluid transit delays to prevent localized chemical over-saturation within the reservoir.
The Telemetry and Communication Task: Compiles all sensor metrics, pump execution logs, and spectral power distributions into compressed JSON packets. These packets are transmitted via an integrated Wi-Fi or cellular transceiver to a cloud-based storage and analytics dashboard using the lightweight MQTT protocol.
Through this comprehensive integration of fluid dynamics, molecular-level ion monitoring, targeted photobiological radiation, and closed-loop algorithmic processing, the apparatus provides a completely autonomous cultivation environment. It minimizes human error, optimizes genetic crop expression, and maximizes resource utilization efficiency.
