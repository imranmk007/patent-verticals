A gate-all-around (GAA) nanosheet transistor architecture addresses short-channel effects (SCEs) that critically degrade device performance at sub-3nm process nodes, including drain-induced barrier lowering (DIBL), threshold voltage roll-off, and subthreshold leakage. The disclosed architecture comprises a vertically stacked plurality of silicon or silicon-germanium nanosheets suspended between source and drain epitaxial regions, wherein a high-κ dielectric layer and a work-function-engineered metal gate electrode conformally surround each nanosheet on all four sides, providing full electrostatic control over the channel potential. An inner spacer formed from a low-permittivity dielectric material is disposed between adjacent gate stacks and source/drain regions to suppress parasitic capacitance and gate-to-drain fringing fields. The all-around gate geometry minimizes the natural length scale of the device, substantially improving the subthreshold swing toward the 60 mV/decade theoretical limit while reducing DIBL below 10 mV/V, thereby enabling continued transistor scaling without commensurate performance degradation.
