The patch illuminates dermal tissue with a tunable near-infrared source spanning 1100–2500 nm, targeting glucose's characteristic overtone absorption bands near 1450 nm, 1730 nm, and 2270 nm. A miniaturized photodetector array captures diffusely reflected spectra, which are processed via partial least squares regression with real-time temperature and motion artifact compensation to isolate the glucose signal from interfering tissue constituents. Unlike electrochemical continuous glucose monitors, the system requires no subcutaneous insertion, no enzymatic reagents, and carries no infection risk, while delivering continuous readings at five-minute intervals with a mean absolute relative difference below 12% across a 70–350 mg/dL clinical range.
